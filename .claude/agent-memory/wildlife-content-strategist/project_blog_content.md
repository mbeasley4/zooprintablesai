Zoo Printables AI blog posts are first-person, authored by Mike Beasley (Cincinnati zoo educator/founder), modeled on edzoocation.com/blogs/news — conversational, family-friendly, "friend who knows animals over coffee" tone.

**Why:** Brand differentiation from corporate/robotic content; EEAT via a real named author (see [[project-zooprintablesai]] auto-memory re: Dr. Maya → Mike Beasley migration).

**How to apply:** Each post maps to Sanity schema fields: title, slug, createDate (YYYY-MM-DD), postedBy, excerpt (150-180 char), seoTitle (60-70 char), metaDescription (150-160 char), featureImage search terms. Body: 900-1400 words, specific opening hook (never "In this article"), 3-5 H2s, at least one list, one blockquote, exactly ONE mid-article Zoo Printables AI free-printable plug (never opening/closing), FAQ-style H3 sections for AEO, emotional/invitational close, no "Conclusion" heading. Contractions throughout. Banned phrases: "delve into," "furthermore," "it's worth noting," "in conclusion," "fascinating journey," "a myriad of."

**Citation-safety convention I followed:** Kept Cincinnati Zoo references to durable, verifiable facts (Cincinnati Zoo & Botanical Garden, Festival of Lights national reputation, HalZOOween). Avoided inventing specific animal ambassador names, exhibit names, or staff so factual claims stay accurate/citation-worthy.

Design tokens for reference: primary green #2D6A4F, accent orange #F4A261.
